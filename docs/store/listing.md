## Summary (short description, one line)

> Close the tabs you've stopped using — and find any page again by what it said.
> 100% on your device.

## Category

**Productivity** (Workflow & Planning, if a sub-category is offered).

## Detailed description

> **Stop managing tabs. Close everything; lose nothing.**
>
> ypuf quietly clears the tabs you've stopped caring about and gives you instant,
> full-text recall of everything you've ever had open. Tabs are open loops — let
> go of what's dead, recall what matters the moment you need it. Everything stays
> on your device.
>
> **Auto-let-go.** ypuf conservatively archives tabs you've left open for days
> and stopped using. They fade with a soft *puff*; the bar gets lighter. There's
> always an instant undo, it learns from what you reopen, and it never touches
> pinned tabs, audio, logins, forms, or sites you protect.
>
> **Recall by content — the safety net.** Every let-go page is indexed by its
> readable text, not just its title. Press Ctrl/Cmd+Shift+K anywhere and search
> by what a page *said* — "that article about the founder who quit Google to
> farm." Faster than re-googling, with content snippets and an "often revisited"
> marker so the pages you lean on rise to the top.
>
> **Snooze a tab.** Send a tab away until later today, this weekend, next week,
> or when you're back at your desk — with a guaranteed return.
>
> **Bring back the set.** Restore a page together with the working set of tabs it
> was open with — resume your whole research session in one click.
>
> **A calm new tab.** A quiet, glanceable board: your recall shelf plus optional
> panels (RSS, prices, top sites). A full keyboard layer for power users.
> Light, dark, and a starlit night mode.
>
> **Privacy is the whole point.** Nothing ypuf records ever leaves your device —
> no servers, no accounts, no analytics, nothing sold or shared. Page text is
> extracted locally and never transmitted. Incognito is never indexed; banking,
> health, and password-manager sites are excluded by default; form inputs are
> never captured. A visible "what's indexed" view and one-click "forget" put you
> in control. Open source (MIT).
>
> Close everything. Trust that nothing important is lost.

## Screenshots & promo — shot list

Capture at the store's required dimensions (see `SUBMISSION.md` for exact px).
Use a populated profile (let go of ~10 varied pages first) and the theme that
photographs best per shot. Suggested 5:

1. **The recall command bar** (Ctrl/Cmd+Shift+K) mid-search, showing a content
   snippet + highlighted match — *the moat.* (Dark or star looks striking.)
2. **The new-tab board** — the ypuf recall shelf + a panel or two, calm and
   designed. (Light, to show the parchment palette.)
3. **Auto-let-go / the puff** — the popup shelf of recently let-go tabs with the
   "let go for you" markers, or a board mid-puff.
4. **Snooze** — the snooze overlay (themed) with the presets.
5. **Privacy** — the "What's indexed" view + forget, captioned "everything stays
   on your device."

**Promo tiles** (if you fill them): the puff mark on a parchment field with the
tagline "close everything; lose nothing." Reuse the `extension/icons/` mark.
